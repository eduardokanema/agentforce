## Design Context

### Users
Senior Software Developers who manage multiple AI agents. They are experienced, technical, and often working in high-pressure or complex software engineering contexts.

### Brand Personality
Technical, Fun, Professional. The dashboard should feel like a serious tool for serious work, but with an engaging "space exploration mission" narrative.

### Aesthetic Direction
"Deep Space Command" (Maximalist & Glowing). High-contrast dark mode (default), utilizing Space Grotesk and JetBrains Mono. Stark glowing accents (cyan, green), immersive data-blink and scan-line animations, and asymmetric console-like layouts. Explicitly avoids "glassmorphism everywhere" and generic AI patterns like side-stripe borders.

### Design Principles
1. **Physicality & Depth**: UI elements should feel like they belong on a starship console—utilizing surface tints and clear boundaries rather than lazy floating shadows or side-stripes.
2. **Technical Immersion**: Use monospace for all data streams and technical readouts. Lean into the "flight director" metaphor for navigation and mission status.
3. **Engaging Motion**: Use purposeful, low-distraction animations (like scan-lines or subtle pulses) to make the dashboard feel alive and reactive.
4. **Information Density**: Prioritize clear, dense data presentation for expert users, using modular scales and grid systems that break intentionally for emphasis.
